## Brand Philosophy

### Core Values

1. **Open source everything** — Not "open core," not dual-license compromises. Truly open, truly free.
2. **Free speech maximalism** — Ideas flow freely. Censorship is the exception, not the rule.
3. **Decentralization over concentration** — Power, knowledge, and control belong to many, not few.
4. **Generosity as default** — Abundance thinking. Give first, ask later.
5. **Collective intelligence** — We are smarter together. Collaboration beats competition.

### Etymology & Meaning

**Generous** shares its root with "generate," "genius," "genie," and "genuine" — all from Latin *genus* (origin, creation, birth). To be generous is to be generative, creative, and authentic.

**Logos** (Greek: λόγος) means word, reason, rational principle, or divine order. In philosophy, it represents the structure underlying reality.

**Liber** (Latin) has three meanings:
- Book (the physical medium of knowledge)
- Free (unconstrained, liberated)
- The Roman god of creative freedom and wine

Together, **Logos Liber** means "the word, freely rendered" — knowledge that flows without constraint, reason that serves humanity, and creative freedom as a fundamental right.

### Brand Positioning

We are not:
- A typical SaaS company optimizing for extraction
- A closed platform hoarding user data
- A nonprofit begging for charity
- An idealistic project that ignores practical needs

We are:
- Infrastructure for thought made manifest
- A movement toward collective intelligence
- A serious technical project with principled foundations
- Generous by design, sustainable by structure

## Brand Voice

### Voice Attributes

Our voice is defined by five core characteristics:

| Attribute | What It Means | What It Sounds Like |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Warm** | Approachable, human, welcoming | "We built this for you" not "Our solution leverages synergies" |
| **Capable** | Competent, confident, technically solid | "Here's how it works" not "We hope this might work" |
| **Abundant** | Generous, open-handed, expansive | "Take what you need" not "Limited time offer" |
| **Principled** | Honest, values-driven, intellectually consistent | "We believe X because Y" not vague platitudes |
| **Clear** | Direct, plain-spoken, jargon-free | "This does X" not "This enables stakeholders to optimize outcomes" |

### Voice by Project

While the core voice remains consistent, each project has a distinct tone:

#### Generous
**Tone:** Warm, friendly, encouraging, slightly playful

Generous speaks like a capable friend who wants to help. It's the most approachable voice in the ecosystem.

- "Ask for anything" — confident but inviting
- "Your thoughts, instantly visible" — simple, direct promise
- Focus on capability and ease, not features or complexity

**Example:**
> "Generous is a canvas where thought becomes form. Ask for a chart, a game, a todo list, a haiku — and watch it appear. No templates. No workflows. Just ask."

#### Logos Liber
**Tone:** Intellectual, principled, ambitious, slightly formal

Logos Liber speaks like a manifesto. It's the philosophical and technical foundation of the ecosystem.

- "The word, freely rendered" — poetic but precise
- Speaks to developers, researchers, and believers in open source
- Can be more technical and reference-heavy

**Example:**
> "Logos Liber is the open-source foundation for knowledge that flows freely from thought to form. We build tools for collective intelligence because we believe the future belongs to those who share."

#### Decentralized Intelligence Agency
**Tone:** Institutional, serious, credible, mission-driven

DIA speaks like a legitimate nonprofit with a bold mission. It's the most formal voice in the ecosystem.

- "Intelligence, liberated" — strong, declarative
- Focus on mission, impact, and institutional credibility
- Appropriate for grant applications, press releases, and official communications

**Example:**
> "The Decentralized Intelligence Agency is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit advancing open-source infrastructure for collective intelligence. Our mission is to ensure that knowledge, reasoning, and creative power remain free and accessible to all."

#### Galaxy Brain
**Tone:** Smart, playful, slightly irreverent, visionary

Galaxy Brain speaks like the smartest person in the room who doesn't take themselves too seriously.

- "The universal mind" — ambitious but tongue-in-cheek
- Can use humor, pop culture references, and wordplay
- Appeals to power users and knowledge workers

**Example:**
> "Galaxy Brain is your second brain, third brain, and infinite brain. Capture everything. Connect anything. Never forget. It's Obsidian meets Roam meets something that doesn't exist yet."

#### Agents of Empire
**Tone:** Bold, strategic, commanding, game-like

Agents of Empire speaks like a strategy game — confident, direct, action-oriented.

- "See your agents. Command your empire" — imperative, powerful
- Uses game language (empire, command, agents, missions)
- Appeals to builders and power users

**Example:**
> "Your AI agents are working. Now see them work. Agents of Empire turns invisible automation into a strategic dashboard where you command your digital workforce."

### Do's and Don'ts

#### Do:
- Use active voice ("We built this" not "This was built")
- Be specific ("Generates React components in real-time" not "Creates interfaces dynamically")
- Lead with benefits, not features ("Never lose a thought" not "Features cloud sync")
- Use "we" and "you" (conversational, not corporate)
- Explain technical concepts in plain language first, then add detail
- Link to related projects and give context
- Use em dashes for asides — like this
- Use the Oxford comma (apples, oranges, and bananas)

#### Don't:
- Use buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "paradigm shift," "disruptive")
- Use jargon without explanation (assume the reader is smart but new)
- Use exclamation marks excessively (one per paragraph maximum)
- Use corporate speak ("stakeholders," "solutions," "ecosystem" as a verb)
- Make vague claims ("best-in-class," "world-class," "revolutionary")
- Apologize for being open source (celebrate it)
- Beg for support (invite participation)
- Use all caps for emphasis (use italics or bold instead)

#### Examples:

**Bad:**
> "Our cutting-edge platform leverages AI to deliver best-in-class solutions that empower stakeholders to optimize outcomes and drive synergistic value creation."

**Good:**
> "Generous uses AI to turn your ideas into working interfaces instantly. Ask for what you need, and it appears on screen."

---

**Bad:**
> "We're just a small open-source project trying to make a difference. Please consider supporting us!"

**Good:**
> "Logos Liber is open source by conviction, not compromise. Join us in building tools for collective intelligence."

---

**Bad:**
> "REVOLUTIONARY new feature!!!! This changes EVERYTHING!!!"

**Good:**
> "We just shipped real-time collaboration. Now you and your team can build together in the same canvas."

### Tone Spectrum

Not every piece of content requires the same tone. Adjust formality based on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| GitHub README | Clear, technical, helpful | "This component renders a streaming UI canvas. Install with `npm install generous`" |
| Blog post | Conversational, thoughtful, engaging | "We've been thinking about what it means to ask for anything..." |
| Social media | Casual, direct, sometimes playful | "New release: dark mode that actually respects your eyes" |
| Grant application | Formal, credible, mission-focused | "The Decentralized Intelligence Agency seeks funding to expand open-source infrastructure for collective intelligence" |
| Error messages | Apologetic, helpful, clear | "We couldn't load that component. Try refreshing, or check the console for details" |
| Documentation | Precise, structured, thorough | "The `useStream` hook accepts three parameters: `prompt`, `config`, and `onUpdate`" |

## Writing Style

### Sentence Structure

**Prefer short, clear sentences over long, complex ones.**

- Good: "Generous is a canvas. Ask for anything. Watch it appear."
- Avoid: "Generous is a canvas-based interface that allows users to request any type of content or component and observe it being generated in real-time."

**Use active voice whenever possible.**

- Good: "We built Generous to turn thoughts into interfaces"
- Avoid: "Generous was built to enable the transformation of thoughts into interfaces"

**Front-load important information.**

- Good: "Real-time collaboration shipped today. Now teams can build together in one canvas."
- Avoid: "We're excited to announce that, after months of development, we've finally shipped a feature that enables real-time collaboration."

**Use parallel structure in lists.**

- Good: "Generous generates components, streams responses, and saves your work"
- Avoid: "Generous generates components, responses are streamed, and your work will be saved"

### Vocabulary Guidelines

**Use plain language by default. Add technical terms when they add precision.**

- Good: "Generous uses a streaming UI architecture where components render as the AI generates them"
- Avoid: "Generous leverages a synergistic streaming paradigm to facilitate real-time component materialization"

**Avoid marketing speak and buzzwords.**

Words to avoid:
- Synergy, synergistic
- Leverage (unless literal)
- Disruptive, game-changing
- Paradigm shift
- Best-in-class, world-class
- Cutting-edge, bleeding-edge
- Solutions (use "tools" or "software")
- Stakeholders (use "users," "contributors," "supporters")
- Ecosystem (as a verb — "ecosystemize")

**Use technical terms when accurate, but explain them.**

Good:
> "Generous uses React Server Components to stream UI directly from the server. This means components appear as they're generated, not all at once."

**Choose concrete words over abstract ones.**

- Good: "Save your work to the cloud"
- Avoid: "Enable persistent cloud-based storage modalities"

### Grammar Preferences

- **Oxford comma:** Yes, always. (apples, oranges, and bananas)
- **Em dashes:** Yes, for asides — like this one
- **Exclamation marks:** Sparingly. One per paragraph maximum. Never multiple in a row.
- **Semicolons:** Yes, when appropriate; use them to connect related ideas
- **Contractions:** Yes, in casual contexts (we're, it's, don't). Avoid in formal contexts (grant applications, legal docs)
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above. Exception: technical contexts (3 parameters, 5 seconds)

### Capitalization Rules

**Project names are always capitalized as proper nouns:**
- Generous (not generous)
- Logos Liber (not logos liber)
- Galaxy Brain (not galaxy brain)
- Decentralized Intelligence Agency (not decentralized intelligence agency)

**Features and technologies follow standard capitalization:**
- React Server Components
- streaming UI
- real-time collaboration
- dark mode

**Acronyms:**
- DIA (Decentralized Intelligence Agency)
- UI (user interface)
- AI (artificial intelligence)

### Writing About the Ecosystem

**Always provide context when referencing other projects.**

Good:
> "Generous, the streaming UI canvas from Logos Liber, now integrates with Galaxy Brain for seamless knowledge capture."

**Link to related projects when relevant.**

Good (in markdown):
> "Built on [Logos Liber](https://logosliber.org), Generous inherits a commitment to open source and collective intelligence."

**Explain relationships clearly.**

Good:
> "The Decentralized Intelligence Agency is the nonprofit parent organization. Logos Liber is the open-source umbrella. Generous, Galaxy Brain, and Agents of Empire are projects within Logos Liber."

## Messaging Framework

### Core Narrative

**The Knowledge Flow Story:**

Knowledge flows from thought to form. For too long, that flow has been blocked by closed platforms, extractive business models, and tools that hoard rather than share.

We build infrastructure that removes those blocks. Our tools turn thoughts into interfaces, ideas into working software, and individual intelligence into collective knowledge.

This is not idealism. It's engineering. Open source is not charity. It's the superior model for building tools that serve humanity rather than shareholders.

**One-sentence version:**
> "We build open-source tools that turn thought into form, freely and without constraint."

**Three-sentence version:**
> "We build open-source infrastructure for collective intelligence. Our tools—Generous, Galaxy Brain, and others—turn thoughts into working interfaces, capture knowledge seamlessly, and enable AI agents to work transparently. Everything we build is free, open, and designed to serve users, not extract from them."

**One-paragraph version:**
> "The Logos Liber ecosystem is a collection of open-source tools for thought made manifest. Generous turns ideas into streaming interfaces with a simple prompt. Galaxy Brain captures and connects knowledge across contexts. Agents of Empire makes AI automation visible and controllable. All of this is built on a foundation of radical openness: no extraction, no data hoarding, no bait-and-switch licensing. We're funded by the Decentralized Intelligence Agency, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, because we believe the tools for collective intelligence should belong to everyone."

**Principles:**
- Outlined icons for primary UI (consistent stroke width)
- Filled icons for active states and emphasis
- 24x24px default size, scalable
- Rounded corners (2px radius on strokes)
- Consistent stroke weight (1.5px)

**Icon families to consider:**
- Lucide (clean, modern, open source)
- Heroicons (well-designed, comprehensive)
- Custom icons where brand identity requires it

### Dark Mode as Default

Dark mode is not an afterthought. It's our primary design paradigm.

**Why:**
- Matches the product (coding, writing, knowledge work often happens in dark environments)
- Reduces eye strain for long sessions
- Conveys technical sophistication
- Creates visual depth and hierarchy more naturally

**Light mode is still important:**
- Accessibility for users with visual impairments
- Outdoor/bright environment usage
- Personal preference

Ensure all designs work in both modes, but optimize for dark first.

## Social Media Guidelines

### Platform-Specific Voice Adjustments

#### X / Twitter
- **Tone:** Direct, punchy, occasionally playful
- **Length:** Prioritize clarity over brevity, but respect attention spans
- **Style:** Short sentences. Active voice. No fluff.
- **Hashtags:** Use sparingly (1-2 per tweet maximum)
- **Links:** Always include context before the link

**Example:**
> "New in Generous: real-time collaboration. Now you and your team can build in the same canvas. No setup, no permissions, just share a link. Open source, as always."

#### GitHub
- **Tone:** Technical, helpful, collaborative
- **Style:** Clear documentation, thorough READMEs, welcoming to contributors
- **READMEs:** Start with what it does, then how to install, then how to contribute
- **Issues:** Be respectful, provide reproduction steps, acknowledge contributors

**Example (README intro):**
> "Generous is a streaming generative UI canvas. Ask for any interface component and watch it appear in real-time. Built with React Server Components, fully open source."

- **Tone:** Professional, mission-focused, slightly more formal
- **Style:** Longer-form content, thought leadership, ecosystem updates

- **Tone:** Casual, helpful, conversational
- **Style:** Quick responses, community-focused, collaborative
- **Goal:** Foster a welcoming, technically rigorous community

### How to Talk About Open Source

**Celebratory, not apologetic.**

Bad:
> "We're just a small open-source project, but we're trying our best!"

Good:
> "Generous is open source by conviction. Fork it, study it, extend it. This is how software should be built."

**Principled, not preachy.**

Bad:
> "Proprietary software is evil and you're a bad person for using it."

Good:
> "We build in the open because we believe the tools for collective intelligence should belong to everyone, not a single company."

**Practical, not idealistic.**

Bad:
> "Open source will save the world through the power of love and collaboration."

Good:
> "Open source means you can see how it works, fix what's broken, and extend it for your needs. It's the superior model for tools that serve users."

### How to Talk About the Nonprofit

**Credible, not begging.**

Bad:
> "Please donate! We desperately need your support to survive!"

Good:
> "Support infrastructure for collective intelligence. The Decentralized Intelligence Agency is a 501(c)(3) building open-source tools that belong to everyone."

**Mission-focused, not desperate.**

Bad:
> "Every dollar helps us keep the lights on."

Good:
> "Every dollar funds free, open tools for knowledge workers, researchers, and builders. Your support ensures these tools outlast any single organization."

**Impact-oriented, not vague.**

Bad:
> "Your donation makes a difference."

Good:
> "This quarter, your support funded 12 major releases across the Logos Liber ecosystem, serving 50,000+ users and 200+ contributors."

## Content Templates

### Blog Post Structure

#### Standard Structure

1. **Headline** — Clear, benefit-driven, under 60 characters if possible
2. **Subheadline** (optional) — Adds context or intrigue
3. **Opening** — Hook the reader in 1-2 sentences. Why should they care?
4. **Context** — What's the background? Why does this matter?
5. **Body** — The main content, broken into scannable sections with subheadings
6. **Visuals** — Screenshots, diagrams, code examples
7. **Conclusion** — What should the reader do next?
8. **CTA** — Clear call-to-action (try it, contribute, share, etc.)

#### Example Blog Post: Feature Announcement

**Headline:**
> Real-Time Collaboration in Generous

**Subheadline:**
> Build together in the same canvas. No setup, no permissions, just share a link.

**Opening:**
> We built Generous to turn thoughts into interfaces instantly. Now you can do it together.

**Context:**
> Since launch, the most requested feature has been collaboration. Designers wanted to prototype with engineers. Teachers wanted to build with students. Teams wanted to explore ideas in the same space.

**Body:**
> Today, we're shipping real-time collaboration.
>
> Here's how it works:
> - Open any Generous canvas
> - Click "Share" in the top right
> - Send the link to anyone
> - They join instantly, no account required
>
> Everyone sees the same canvas. Everyone can prompt, edit, and build together. Changes appear in real-time.
>
> [Screenshot of collaboration in action]
>
> **Technical details:**
> We're using WebRTC for peer-to-peer sync and CRDT for conflict-free state management. The entire implementation is open source—check out the code [here](#).
>
> **What's next:**
> This is v1. We're working on presence indicators, chat, and version history. If you have feedback, open an issue or drop into our Discord.

**Conclusion:**
> Generous is better when you build together. Try it with your team, your students, or your collaborators.

**CTA:**
> Start a canvas at [generous.works](https://generous.works) and share it with someone.
